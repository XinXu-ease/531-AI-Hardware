# 531-AI-Hardware
Smart Adaptive Chair

Critique & Redesign of an Everyday Object

Overview

This project critiques a common home/office chair and proposes a sensor- and ML-enabled redesign. While many chairs are adjustable, they remain static and reactive, relying on users to notice discomfort and manually intervene. This redesign explores how a chair could become aware of user state, adapt to different users and tasks, and intervene before discomfort appears.

Identified Gaps

Awareness Gap
Chairs do not sense who is sitting on them, how the user is sitting, or whether their current configuration is supportive. Users usually notice problems only after discomfort or pain occurs.

Adaptation Gap
Chairs assume one setup fits all. They do not adapt when different people use the same chair or when one user switches tasks (laptop, writing, desktop work), and users rarely readjust settings proactively.

Anticipation Gap
Chairs are reactive rather than proactive. They wait for users to feel discomfort instead of identifying unhealthy patterns as they gradually develop.

Redesign Proposal

Design Rationale
The redesign addresses awareness, adaptation, and anticipation to improve everyday comfort and reduce long-term musculoskeletal risk.

Proposed Features
The chair integrates sensors in the seat pan and backrest to sense pressure distribution and upper-body support. Motorized actuators adjust the backrest, seat height, and armrests. A machine learning model personalizes interpretation and intervention, since comfort and unhealthy patterns vary by body type.

Social Considerations
The system is designed to be mostly invisible and fit existing habits. After a short learning period, it adapts quietly through subtle adjustments or gentle reminders, with minimal social cost or need for explanation.

Tradeoffs
The redesign increases cost and complexity and introduces potential sensing or prediction errors. However, leaving chairs static leads to delayed feedback and repeated discomfort. Interventions are kept reversible and optional to maintain user trust.
